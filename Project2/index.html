This project highlights women writers from the Middle East and North Africa. It serves as a compilation of names of women writers, who should be read and celebrated for their brillian work and their resistance. This project presents to you their names, their stories and their work.

As a Tunisian woman, I always challenge myself to seek books and poems by Arab and Amazigh women writers to read. This project is a reminder for everyone to read their work. Thir works are a testamant to their talent, strengh and resiliency in the face patriarchy, occupation and colonialism.

I thought extensively before choosing a name for this project. Referring to these women as Arab women writers won't do justice to the Amazigh origins of people in North Africa and will ignore the colonial invasion of the region by the Arabs. I chose to call this project "Women Writers from the Middle and North Africa" to highlight the diversity of women from the region. Some identify as Arab, some as Amazigh, some as Berber and some as North African.

This project is an Open Source repository of names of women writers. This is in no way an exhaustive list and I encourage everyone to get in contact with me if they have suggestions of names to include. To do so, please fill out this form.
